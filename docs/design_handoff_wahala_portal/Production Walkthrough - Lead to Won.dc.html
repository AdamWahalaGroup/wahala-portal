Opportunity → Won: a script to type into production

Do these steps yourself, in order, in the real app. Each step names the exact screen, the exact button, and what you should type. After each step there's a ✓ Expect line — if what you see doesn't match, that's a disconnect: write down the step number and what actually happened.

Before you start — this rev matches the single-pipeline model: there is no separate leads inbox or Triage column anymore. Everything is an opportunity attached to a contact from day one; accepting it into Discovery starts the deal. Use an obviously fake test name so you can find/delete it after (this script uses "ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs" and contact "Jordan Blake" throughout — swap in your own if you like, just stay consistent). You'll need two browser windows: one signed in as staff, one incognito/private (to view the client-facing proposal link with no session). In the staff sidebar, make sure Training mode is ON — this test deliberately exercises its nudges.

1 · Create the opportunity

Screen: Opportunities board (/dashboard/sales) → "+ New opportunity".

In the modal:

  • Contact: create new — Name: Jordan Blake, Email: jordan@fenwicklabs.com, Company: ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs
  • "What do they need": Needs a scheduling tool for a 12-person field crew, paper-based today. (this seeds the opportunity name and the account dossier — don't skip it)
  • Est. value: 42000
  • Source: pick anything (e.g. "Referral")

Click Create opportunity →.

✓ Expect: a new card lands in the New stage of the pipeline, named from the account + your need sentence, value $42,000. Check Contacts: Jordan Blake exists with 1 opportunity. No deal is "started" yet — New holds opportunities you haven't accepted.

It's also valid to create the opportunity with a contact only and no company — the account is born automatically when the deal is Won. If you want to test that path too, run this script a second time leaving Company blank.

2 · Accept it — start the deal

Have the first call (role-play it, or actually call a colleague). You learn there's a real need — worth pursuing.

Screen: click the card in New to open its drawer → "Accept → start Discovery" (not Lost/Pass).

✓ Expect: the opportunity moves to the Discovery stage, owned by you, value $42,000. Nothing converts or gets deleted — the same record changed stage, and Jordan/the account are still attached. There is no separate "lead" object anywhere.

3 · Discovery — fill the package, watch readiness

Screen: the deal drawer → Discovery package section. On a fresh deal it shows all 10 fields with a red ✗ and a NOT SCORED pill: Business profile, Current workflow, Pain points, Budget posture, Decision makers, Success metrics, MVP priorities, Timeline, Customer terminology, Deferred scope.

Below the fields, check the "Ask on the next call" list — one suggested question per unfilled field (e.g. Business profile → "What does the business actually do, in their words?"). Also check the Next step card above it reads something like "Capture the discovery call — requirements included — then draft the proposal." These two are your call prep: use them on the discovery call.

Have the call and get its content onto the deal — in production the Package extractor agent reads a recorded call and fills the fields; you can also fill any field by hand: click the field → its editor opens with three status chips — ok / partial / missing — and an evidence box ("What did they say? (evidence)") → pick a status, paste what they actually said, Save. Use real sentences, e.g. for Current workflow: Fenwick runs crew scheduling on a shared spreadsheet. Jordan wants a mobile-friendly tool dispatchers and field crew can both use. Vague evidence makes vague proposals — the AI draft in step 4 reads from this.

For this test, hand-fill a spread: mark ~5 fields ok with evidence, 1–2 partial, leave the rest missing.

✓ Expect: a saved ok field flips its ✗ to a green check and its question disappears from "Ask on the next call" — only green/completed fields drop off; partial and missing fields keep their question in the list. NOT SCORED becomes a readiness score and the Next step line updates. Partial fields should count for something but not read as done. With that spread the score should land below 7/10 — not proposal-ready, which is what step 4's nudge test needs. If a field saves but its check/score/ask-list don't move together, that's a disconnect.

4 · Draft & send the proposal

There is no "Move to Proposal out" button — the stage follows the proposal. The deal stays in Discovery while you draft; it moves to Proposal out automatically the moment you hit Send. If you see a manual "Move to Proposal out" action anywhere, that's a disconnect.

In the deal drawer, click "◆ Rough out a draft" (or "+ Blank proposal" if you'd rather type it by hand). If prompted, choose "Standard vs. phased" (2 paths).

Open the proposal editor and fill in real numbers on at least one option — name it, price it (e.g. $42,000), and if it's the phased option, give it 2 phases with amounts/weeks that add up.

Click Send.

Watch for: with training mode on and readiness under 7, Send must pop the "not proposal-ready" nudge — "Hold the send" or "Send anyway" (override logged to the deal; the nudge is never a hard gate). Pick Send anyway to keep moving. If no nudge appears at sub-7 readiness with training mode on, that's a disconnect.

✓ Expect: proposal status flips to Sent, the deal moves to Proposal out on its own (check the board — no manual stage click), and the editor shows the public share link ("the prospect reads & signs here", wahala.co/p/…). Also check the Proposals page: your proposal appears there as a row belonging to this deal — proposals are not a pipeline of their own.

5 · Play the client — sign it

Copy the share link, open it in your incognito window (no login). Pick an option, type Jordan Blake under "Type your full name to sign", click Sign & approve.

✓ Expect: a "Signed & sealed" confirmation with the phase roadmap. Back in staff view, the deal should now be in Committed — not still sitting in Proposal out or Negotiating. If it didn't move, that's a disconnect to flag.

6 · Committed — agreements & deposit

Open the deal drawer again. You should see an Agreement package checklist and a "When the deposit clears" card listing the phases that will become the project.

Watch for: this seam used to be the riskiest spot in the loop, and it now has defined behavior — a brand-new account must get a default agreement package seeded at Committed (MSA, NDA, commercial agreement, and a deposit invoice ≈10% of deal value, ~$4,200 here). If the checklist renders empty, the phase list under "When the deposit clears" is empty/zero, or Create project stays greyed out with no way to mark the deposit received — stop and report it exactly here. Also: a proposal signed without explicit phases should still produce one "Full engagement" phase at the deal value, never zero phases.

Mark the deposit invoice as received/cleared (however production exposes that — an admin action, a Stripe webhook in real life, or a manual "mark paid" toggle if Stripe isn't wired up yet).

Click Create project →.

7 · Won — verify the landing

You should land on a brand-new project page for ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs.

Check, in order:

  • The project has the phase(s) from the signed proposal — right names, right amounts — not empty, not generic.
  • Phase 1 opens already paid (the deposit is Phase 1's payment — you should not be asked to pay it twice).
  • The deal is gone from the pipeline — it should not still show anywhere in New/Discovery/Proposal out/Negotiating/Committed.
  • Go to Accounts → ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs: the account should now say Client, not Prospect, and its timeline should show the whole thread — opportunity, discovery, proposal, signed, committed, won — as one continuous history, not a gap.
  • On Jordan Blake's contact page you should see portal status and a Send invite action — this is the moment the client gets their own login.

If all five hold, the loop closed end-to-end — opportunity to Won, no dead ends.

Known interim states — not bugs

  • Payment: if Stripe checkout isn't live yet, "deposit cleared" may be an admin-only manual toggle rather than a real card payment. The gate (can't create the project until it's marked cleared) should still be enforced.
  • Readiness extraction: the Package extractor agent only fills discovery fields from recorded calls. If call recording isn't connected, filling the package manually is the expected fallback — the readiness score and the sub-7 nudge should behave identically either way.
  • Contact email invite: creating a contact with an email may fire a portal invite immediately ("Create + send invite"). For this test that's fine — the invite goes to a fake address; just don't use a real client's email.
  • Zoom: scheduling/logging calls works without Zoom connected — you'll see a "no video link, paste one manually" state instead of a Join button. That's expected, not broken.
  • Contract/SOW: generating the linked Contract/SOW document is a separate action from the proposal itself (from the proposal editor, "Generate contract / SOW") — it's not required to reach Won, but if you want to test it too, do it right after step 4, before sending.
Clean-up: once you're done, archive the "ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs" account (Account page → Archive) rather than leaving it live — archive hides it, deletes nothing. Then turn Training mode back to whatever your team runs with.
