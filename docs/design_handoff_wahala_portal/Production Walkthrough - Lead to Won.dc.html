Lead → Won: a script to type into production

Do these steps yourself, in order, in the real app. Each step names the exact screen, the exact button, and what you should type. After each step there's a ✓ Expect line — if what you see doesn't match, that's a disconnect: write down the step number and what actually happened.

Before you start — use an obviously fake test name so you can find/delete it after (this script uses "ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs" and contact "Jordan Blake", throughout — swap in your own if you like, just stay consistent). You'll need two browser windows: one signed in as staff, one incognito/private (to view the client-facing proposal link with no session).

1 · Get the lead — capture the contact

Screen: Sales board (/dashboard/sales) → "+ Capture contact" button.

Type in:

  • Name: Jordan Blake
  • Email: jordan@fenwicklabs.com
  • Account: type ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs, use "+ create new" (this is a brand-new account, not an existing one)
  • Source: pick anything (e.g. "Referral")
  • Est. value: 42000
  • "What they need": a sentence or two — e.g. Needs a scheduling tool for a 12-person field crew, paper-based today.

Leave the three qualification chips unchecked — you haven't talked to them yet. Click Save to Triage.

✓ Expect: the modal closes, and a new card for Jordan Blake appears in the Triage column of the board. No deal exists yet — Triage holds contacts, not deals.

2 · The phone call — qualify into a deal

Have the call (role-play it, or actually call a colleague). On the call you learn: they have a real need, and a budget signal, but no confirmed decision-maker yet — 2 of 3.

Screen: back on the board, find Jordan Blake's card in Triage → click Qualify (not Pass).

✓ Expect: the contact leaves Triage, and a new deal appears in the Discovery column, named after the account, owned by you, value $42,000. This is the moment "lead" stops being a separate thing — Jordan is now a qualified Contact, ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs is an Account, and there's an open Deal. Nothing converts or gets deleted; the same contact record just changed state.

Faster alternative for a warm lead: at capture, check 2+ of the qualification chips right away and click "Start deal → Discovery" instead of Save to Triage — contact, account, and deal are created in one step. Use this if the call already happened before you sat down to log it.

3 · Log the call on the deal

Screen: click into the new deal to open its drawer → footer → "Log a call".

Enter a short discovery note — what they actually need, in their words, e.g. Fenwick runs crew scheduling on a shared spreadsheet. Jordan wants a mobile-friendly tool dispatchers and field crew can both use.

✓ Expect: the call is logged to the deal's activity/history, and the discovery note is attached to the deal (this note is what the AI proposal draft reads from later — vague notes make vague proposals).

4 · Move to Proposal out & draft the proposal

In the deal drawer, advance the stage: Discovery → Proposal out.

Watch for: with training mode on, moving to Proposal out below a readiness threshold pops a "not proposal-ready" nudge. That's intended — either "Keep in Discovery" or "Advance anyway" (logged). Either is fine for this test; pick Advance anyway to keep moving.

Still in the drawer, click "◆ Rough out a draft" (or "+ Blank proposal" if you'd rather type it by hand). If prompted, choose "Standard vs. phased" (2 paths).

Once drafted, open the proposal editor and fill in real numbers on at least one option — name it, price it (e.g. $42,000), and if it's the phased option, give it 2 phases with amounts/weeks that add up.

Click Send.

✓ Expect: proposal status flips to Sent, and you get a public share link (wahala.co/p/…).

5 · Play the client — sign it

Copy the share link, open it in your incognito window (no login). Pick an option, type Jordan Blake as the signer name, click Sign & approve.

✓ Expect: a "Signed & sealed" confirmation. Back in staff view, the deal should now be in Committed — not still sitting in Proposal out or Negotiating. If it didn't move, that's a disconnect to flag.

6 · Committed — agreements & deposit

Open the deal drawer again. You should see an Agreement package checklist (MSA, NDA, commercial agreement, deposit invoice) and a "When the deposit clears" card listing the phases that will become the project.

Watch for: this is the single riskiest spot in the whole loop — a brand-new account (like this test one) has never had agreements before. If the checklist renders empty, or the phase list under "When the deposit clears" is empty/zero, or Create project stays greyed out with no way to mark the deposit received — stop and report it exactly here. That's the seam between "proposal signed" and "becomes a real project," and it's the one place a system stitched together from many small changes is most likely to drop the thread.

Mark the deposit invoice as received/cleared (however production exposes that — an admin action, a Stripe webhook in real life, or a manual "mark paid" toggle if Stripe isn't wired up yet).

Click Create project →.

7 · Won — verify the landing

You should land on a brand-new project page for ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs.

Check, in order:

  • The project has the phase(s) from the signed proposal — right names, right amounts — not empty, not generic.
  • Phase 1 opens already paid (the deposit is Phase 1's payment — you should not be asked to pay it twice).
  • The deal is gone from the Sales board — it should not still show anywhere in Discovery/Proposal out/Negotiating/Committed.
  • Go to Accounts → ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs: the account should now say Client, not Prospect, and its timeline should show the whole thread — capture, call, proposal, signed, committed, won — as one continuous history, not a gap.
  • You should be prompted (or able) to invite Jordan Blake to the portal — this is the moment the client gets their own login.

If all five hold, the loop closed end-to-end — lead to Won, no dead ends.

Known interim states — not bugs

  • Payment: if Stripe checkout isn't live yet, "deposit cleared" may be an admin-only manual toggle rather than a real card payment. The gate (can't create the project until it's marked cleared) should still be enforced.
  • Zoom: "Log a call" / "Schedule call" work without Zoom connected — you'll see a "no video link, paste one manually" state instead of a Join button. That's expected, not broken.
  • Contract/SOW: generating the linked Contract/SOW document is a separate action from the proposal itself (from the proposal editor, "Generate contract / SOW") — it's not required to reach Won, but if you want to test it too, do it right after step 4, before sending.
Clean-up: once you're done, archive the "ZZ Test — Fenwick Labs" account (Account page → Archive) rather than leaving it live — archive hides it, deletes nothing.
