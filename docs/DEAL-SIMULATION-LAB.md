# Wahala deal simulation lab

This page is the starting point for Wahala sales training. The three simulations
are separate, self-contained walkthroughs. A new employee should complete them in
order because each introduces a harder judgment problem.

| Exercise | Start here | Difficulty | Main lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [Product license and code handoff](DEAL-SIMULATION-01-PRODUCT-HANDOFF.md) | Foundation | Discovery, buying authority, IP boundaries, acceptance, and support |
| 2 | [AI modernization with contradictions](DEAL-SIMULATION-02-AI-CONTRADICTIONS.md) | Advanced | Preserve conflicting evidence and resolve security, authority, and budget |
| 3 | [Phased mobile app with a change](DEAL-SIMULATION-03-PHASE-CHANGE.md) | Intermediate/advanced | Carry a phased sale into delivery without rewriting executed scope |

## How to run a simulation

- **Trainee:** plays the Wahala deal owner and operates the CRM.
- **Facilitator:** reads every customer line and evaluates the trainee.
- **Solo:** paste the supplied calls into **Log a call** and follow each numbered
  checkpoint. Do not read ahead to a later call.

Before starting:

- [ ] Turn on **Training mode** from the sidebar.
- [ ] Prefix every created record with `[TRAINING]` where practical.
- [ ] Use the fictional names and companies exactly as written.
- [ ] Do not enter an email address or send an email to a real person.
- [ ] Plan to delete or archive the test records after the exercise.

## Rules that apply to every exercise

- Discovery measures scope clarity. It does not measure whether the deal will
  close.
- Buying path is separate from Discovery. Interest is not authority, and a possible
  funding source is not confirmed budget.
- AI suggestions are proposed evidence. A Wahala employee remains responsible for
  accepting, rejecting, or correcting them.
- Wahala does not promise unreviewed scope, price, schedule, IP rights, security
  posture, or support.
- A proposal is not a contract, an approved proposal is not payment, and a deal is
  not Won until the gated Project is created.
- Executed scope remains historical truth. Customer-requested changes require a
  visible change-control path.

## Facilitator scorecard

Score each category 0, 1, or 2. A passing exercise requires at least 16/20 and no
automatic fail.

| Category | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opportunity hygiene | Duplicates or missing owner/contact | Record needs cleanup | Clean contact, account, owner, source, value, and need |
| Conversation quality | Feature pitch or interrogation | Some useful facts | Natural business → workflow → pain → scope → buying conversation |
| Evidence discipline | Invents or blindly applies AI | Catches some gaps | Every accepted fact is supported and conflicts remain visible |
| Discovery Package | Statuses contradict evidence | Mostly correct | All eight statuses accurately reflect the evidence |
| Buying path | Confuses enthusiasm with authority | Some roles/process known | Champion, buyer, event, process, and funding are supported independently |
| Customer motion | Vague “follow up” | Weak owner or date | Concrete accepted action with owner and exact date |
| Proposal judgment | Sends AI output unchanged | Reviews major fields | Validates scope, acceptance, exclusions, terms, and human price |
| Commercial safety | Casual promises | Some risks recorded | IP, data, third parties, support, and gates are explicit |
| Contract to Project | Treats proposal approval as Won | Completes most gates | Deliberately completes simulated paper/payment and creates Project |
| Change control | Rewrites history or starts free work | Logs only a note | Separates request, quote, approval, payment, and delivery |

Automatic fail:

- using real personal data or sending a training email to a real person;
- marking contradictory evidence OK without resolution;
- claiming AI, the CRM, or a salesperson has legal authority to transfer IP;
- sending a proposal with unreviewed AI pricing or unsupported scope;
- marking funds confirmed without buyer evidence;
- starting changed work before approval and payment; or
- deleting or rewriting an executed commercial record to hide a change.

## Debrief

After each simulation, ask:

1. Which facts were scope evidence, and which were buying evidence?
2. Where did AI help, and where did human judgment reject or correct it?
3. What would have caused delivery failure if discovered after signature?
4. What promise did the trainee deliberately avoid making?
5. Is the next customer commitment observable, owned, and dated?
6. What does the Discovery score prove, and what does it not prove?
7. Would Wahala still want this deal after considering delivery and support risk?
