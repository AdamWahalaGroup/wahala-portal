# Wahala operating model

This is the canonical product intent distilled from the founders' recorded
sessions and the first full CRM review. It separates durable business principles
from whiteboard formulas that still need real-world evidence.

## What Wahala is building

Wahala is an engineering consulting company that can sell several shapes of
work:

- a product license or code-fork handoff with deployment enablement;
- modernization of an existing product, including responsible AI adoption;
- a custom web or mobile product build;
- paid discovery or technical assessment;
- advisory/time-and-materials work; and
- ongoing support or a managed service.

The portal is Wahala's operating system for winning and delivering that work. It
protects customer trust and scarce founder attention by preserving evidence,
commitments, commercial decisions, payment state, delivery acceptance, and
postmortems in one account thread.

Wahala employees are invited deliberately. A **Sales rep** can work only the
opportunities and Deals assigned to them: discovery, buying-path evidence,
meetings, proposals, and client follow-up. They cannot confirm received payment
or create the delivery Project. An **Account owner** manages the full customer
relationship across that account. Training mode is a temporary coaching setting,
not a security role. Full Wahala admin access is reserved for people who need
global commercial, configuration, and team-management authority.

## Durable principles

1. Bootstrap with customer revenue; reusable engineering should create margin.
2. Never allow a customer or Wahala commitment to disappear silently.
3. Capture evidence from conversations instead of relying on founder memory.
4. Walk away from weak opportunities before spending delivery-level effort.
5. Keep customer promises explicit: scope, IP, data handling, schedule, support,
   payment, acceptance, and handoff.
6. Use AI aggressively for bounded interpretation and drafting, not authority.
7. Preserve human accountability. A suggestion is not a committed next action.
8. Price value, risk, rights, and responsibility honestly; do not misrepresent
   underlying effort or operating cost.
9. Keep legal, security, payment, and delivery gates stronger than sales stages.
10. Treat every formula as a hypothesis until real deal outcomes validate it.

## The Deal aggregate

One Deal represents one potential commercial engagement. It owns or links to:

- account, contacts, owner, and provenance;
- buying-path evidence;
- Discovery Package evidence;
- one explicit agreed follow-up or scheduled next meeting;
- meetings, activity, and grounded AI suggestions;
- proposals and commercial options;
- agreement, IP, data-risk, payment, and support decisions;
- the closed outcome and resulting Project.

“Opportunity” and “deal in progress” are lifecycle views of this same object.

## Independent decision signals

| Signal | Question it answers |
|---|---|
| Buying path | Can this customer credibly champion, approve, and fund the purchase? |
| Discovery Package | Do we understand enough to scope and price responsibly? |
| Wahala fit | Is this attractive work for Wahala? |
| Engagement health | Is the relationship active and reciprocal? |
| Action urgency | What commitment needs attention now? |
| Capacity feasibility | Can Wahala responsibly accept and deliver it? |

Do not multiply these into a single “truth score.” A silent proposal can have
declining health and increasing action urgency at the same time.

## AI operating policy

Deterministic code owns dates, SLAs, gates, spend ceilings, permissions, and
state transitions. AI may extract cited evidence, expose contradictions, draft
questions, summarize meetings, research with sources, and draft commercial
artifacts for human review.

Model routing and agent budgets must be evaluated against real examples. A
scheduled run is justified by changed evidence or a specific decision—not by a
desire to keep an agent looking busy.

The “Master Money Agent,” automated capacity knobs, token-based project pricing,
and automatic opportunity punts remain experiments, not current product truth.

## Commercial boundaries

Every product/code transfer deal must explicitly choose IP disposition,
deployment ownership, third-party account ownership, warranty, ongoing support,
data responsibilities, and acceptance criteria. Reusable background IP should
not be assigned accidentally.

One-shot and phased delivery use the same Project model. One-shot work has one
paid delivery stage; phased work has several. Payment schedule is a separate
commercial choice.
